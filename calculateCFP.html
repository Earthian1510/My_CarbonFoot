Question: Are you willing to share responsibility for CO₂ emissions by Indian Livestock?

Every year, Indian livestock contributes around 20,000 crore kilograms of CO2 emissions. This livestock primarily includes buffalo and cattle. Consequently, the milk we drink, the curd we enjoy, the butter we consume, and even the meat we eat, all contribute to these emissions. Given that a significant portion of our population is undernourished and relies on these products for essential nutrition, it is crucial to balance our environmental responsibilities. As young citizens of this country, it is our duty to share the responsibility for these emissions on behalf of our fellow citizens.
